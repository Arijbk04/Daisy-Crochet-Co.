Materials

  • Worsted weight yarn (or yarn of your choice)
  • 5mm (H-8) crochet hook
  • Yarn needle
  • Scissors
  • Stitch markers
  • Buttons (optional)

Instructions

Back Panel

Start with a foundation chain. Chain 72 (or adjust based on desired size).
Row 1: Dc in 4th chain from the hook, and in each chain across. (Total 70 dc) Turn.
Rows 2-6: Ch 3 (counts as first dc), skip the first dc, dc in next dc, chain 1, skip 1 dc, repeat across. End with dc in last dc. Turn.
Repeat Row 2 until the panel is about 18 inches tall (or desired length). Fasten off.

Front Panels (Make 2)

Start with a foundation chain of 36 (or adjust based on size).
Row 1: Dc in 4th chain from the hook, and in each chain across (Total 34 dc). Turn.
Rows 2-6: Ch 3, dc in next dc, chain 1, skip 1 dc, repeat across. Turn.
Repeat Row 2 until the panel reaches the same height as the back panel. Fasten off.

Sleeves (Make 2)

Start with a foundation chain of 30 (or adjust based on desired size).
Row 1: Dc in 4th chain from the hook, and in each chain across (Total 28 dc). Turn.
Rows 2-4: Ch 3, dc in next dc, chain 1, skip 1 dc, repeat across. Turn.
Repeat Row 2 until the sleeve is about 15 inches long (or desired length). Fasten off.

Assembly

1. Lay the back panel flat.
2. Attach the front panels to the sides, leaving space for the armholes.
3. Attach the sleeves to the armholes.
4. Use a whip stitch or slip stitch to sew the side seams and sleeve seams.

Edging

For a neat finish, add a border of sc (single crochet) along the front edges and around the neckline.
Optional: Add a scallop or picot stitch for a decorative border.
If adding buttons, attach them to one side of the front panel and make corresponding buttonholes on the other side.

Finishing

Weave in all loose ends and block the cardigan to smooth out any unevenness.
